# Self-Care and Daily Wellbeing

Self-care is not about luxury or indulgence — it is about consistently attending to your basic needs so that you can function and feel well. When life gets busy or difficult, self-care is often the first thing to slip, but it is also when you need it most.

## The Basics Matter Most

Before reaching for elaborate routines, check in with the fundamentals:

- **Sleep**: Are you getting enough rest? Even small improvements in sleep habits can significantly affect mood and energy.
- **Nutrition**: Are you eating regularly? Skipping meals or relying heavily on processed food can affect how you feel both physically and emotionally.
- **Hydration**: Something as simple as drinking enough water through the day can affect your energy and concentration.
- **Movement**: Any form of physical activity, even gentle, helps regulate mood and reduce tension.

## Emotional Self-Care

- **Name your feelings**: Simply putting a name to what you are feeling ("I feel anxious" rather than "I feel bad") can reduce the intensity of the emotion. This is sometimes called affect labelling.
- **Allow yourself to feel**: Not every difficult emotion needs to be fixed immediately. Sometimes sitting with a feeling, acknowledging it, and letting it pass is enough.
- **Journaling**: Writing down your thoughts — even just a few sentences — can help you process what you are going through and notice patterns over time.
- **Set boundaries**: Saying no to things that drain you and yes to things that nourish you is an act of self-care.

## Social Self-Care

- Spend time with people who make you feel safe and accepted.
- It is okay to step back from relationships or social obligations that consistently leave you feeling worse.
- Ask for help when you need it. Needing support is not a weakness.

## Finding What Works for You

Self-care is personal. What works for one person may not work for another. The key is to pay attention to what genuinely helps you feel better (not just what looks good on a list) and to build small, sustainable habits rather than dramatic overhauls.

## A Note on Difficult Days

On days when everything feels too much, self-care might look like doing the bare minimum — having a glass of water, taking a shower, getting through the day. That counts. You do not need to be productive or optimistic every day to be taking care of yourself.

*This is general wellbeing information, not clinical advice.*
