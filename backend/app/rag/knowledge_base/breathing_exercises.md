# Breathing Exercises for Calm

Breathing exercises are one of the simplest and most accessible ways to calm your nervous system. When you feel stressed, anxious, or overwhelmed, your breathing tends to become shallow and rapid. Deliberate slow breathing signals to your body that it is safe to relax.

## Box Breathing (4-4-4-4)

Used by many people to manage stress and stay focused:

- Breathe in slowly through your nose for 4 seconds.
- Hold your breath gently for 4 seconds.
- Breathe out slowly through your mouth for 4 seconds.
- Hold again for 4 seconds.
- Repeat for 4 to 8 rounds.

## 4-7-8 Breathing

This technique emphasises a longer exhale, which can be particularly calming:

- Breathe in through your nose for 4 seconds.
- Hold your breath for 7 seconds.
- Exhale slowly through your mouth for 8 seconds.
- Repeat 3 to 4 times.

If holding your breath feels uncomfortable, shorten the counts proportionally (such as 2-3-4).

## Belly Breathing (Diaphragmatic Breathing)

- Place one hand on your chest and the other on your belly.
- Breathe in slowly through your nose, allowing your belly to rise while your chest stays relatively still.
- Breathe out slowly through your mouth, feeling your belly fall.
- Continue for 5 to 10 minutes.

## Tips for Getting Started

- You do not need a quiet room or special equipment. You can practice anywhere — at your desk, on public transport, or lying in bed.
- Start with just one or two minutes if it feels new. Build up gradually.
- If you feel lightheaded, return to your normal breathing rhythm and try again later with gentler counts.
- Consistency matters more than duration. A minute of mindful breathing every day is more useful than an occasional long session.

*This is general wellbeing information, not clinical advice. If you experience persistent breathing difficulties or panic attacks, please consult a qualified health professional.*
