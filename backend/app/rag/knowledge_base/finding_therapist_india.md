# Finding a Therapist in India

If you feel you would benefit from professional mental health support, seeking a therapist or counselor is a positive step. Here is practical guidance for finding help in India.

## Where to Start

### Government Resources
- **NIMHANS (National Institute of Mental Health and Neurosciences)**, Bengaluru: One of India's leading mental health institutions. They offer outpatient services and can provide referrals. Website: nimhans.ac.in
- **District Mental Health Programme (DMHP)**: Many districts have government-run mental health services at district hospitals. Ask at your nearest government hospital about mental health outpatient services.
- **Tele MANAS**: The Government of India's tele-mental health service, available 24/7 in multiple languages. Call 14416 or 1-800-891-4416 for a free initial consultation and referral.

### Online Directories
- **Practo** and **1mg**: Allow you to search for psychiatrists and psychologists by city, read patient reviews, and book appointments online.
- **iCall** (by TISS, Mumbai): Offers affordable counselling services and can help connect you with professionals. Website: icallhelpline.org
- **Amaha** (formerly InnerHour): Provides online therapy sessions with licensed therapists.
- **MindPeers**, **BetterLYF**, **YourDOST**: Online platforms offering therapy and counselling.

## What to Consider

- **Psychologist vs. Psychiatrist**: A psychologist provides talk therapy and counselling. A psychiatrist is a medical doctor who can also prescribe medication. Many people benefit from one or both.
- **Cost**: Government hospital services and helplines like Tele MANAS are free or very low cost. Private therapists typically charge between Rs 500 and Rs 3000 per session, depending on the city and the practitioner's experience. Some offer sliding scale fees.
- **Online vs. In-Person**: Online therapy has become widely available and can be a convenient option, especially if there are limited mental health professionals in your area.
- **Language**: Look for a therapist who can communicate in a language you are most comfortable in. Many therapists in India offer sessions in Hindi, English, and regional languages.

## What to Expect in a First Session

- The therapist will likely ask you about what brought you to seek help, your general background, and your goals.
- You do not need to share everything at once. Go at your own pace.
- It is okay to try a few therapists before finding the right fit. The relationship between you and your therapist matters.
- Therapy is not a sign of weakness — it is a practical tool for understanding yourself better and building skills to navigate life's challenges.

## Helpline Numbers (24/7)

- **Tele MANAS**: 14416 or 1-800-891-4416 (Govt. of India, multilingual)
- **KIRAN**: 1800-599-0019 (Govt. of India)
- **Vandrevala Foundation**: 1860-266-2345 / 1800-233-3330 / +91 9999 666 555

*This is general guidance, not a substitute for professional advice.*
